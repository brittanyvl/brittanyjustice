Work

Decision systems for compound and sterile drug markets

Longitudinal Monitoring of 503B Pharmacy Compliance Signals

Turning volatile regulatory disclosures into historical intelligence for procurement decisions.

Context / Risk

503B outsourcing facilities operate under FDA oversight, but compliance data is released in formats that overwrite history. Procurement teams making multi-year sourcing decisions had no way to track inspection patterns, warning letters, or registration changes over time.

Why Existing Approaches Failed

The FDA publishes registration data as point-in-time snapshots with no versioning. Commercial databases aggregate but don't preserve historical state. Manual tracking via spreadsheets couldn't scale or provide auditability.

System Design

Built an automated pipeline that captures weekly FDA data releases, applies SCD Type 2 tracking to preserve historical state, and surfaces changes through a monitoring dashboard. The system normalizes facility identifiers across inconsistent source formats and links inspection events to registration records.

Decisions Enabled

  • Identify facilities with deteriorating compliance trajectories before public warning letters
  • Compare inspection frequency and outcomes across potential suppliers
  • Support due diligence for contract renewals with auditable compliance history

Who This Was For

Pharmacy procurement leaders and supply chain teams at health systems and GPOs evaluating 503B outsourcing relationships.
